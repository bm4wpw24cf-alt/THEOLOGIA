Our Mission

Why Beyond TULIP Exists

Our mission is to encourage believers to examine every doctrine in the light of Scripture, with a special focus on the doctrine of salvation (soteriology). We are committed to careful biblical exegesis, historical research, and respectful theological dialogue.

Rather than promoting any theological system as our final authority, we seek to let Scripture speak for itself and to present a clear, faithful, and biblically grounded understanding of the gospel, salvation by grace through faith in Jesus Christ, and the person and work of Christ.

Our desire is to equip Christians to study God's Word with discernment, confidence, and humility, always giving Scripture the highest authority.

Our Doctrine

Statement of Faith

This site is built on the conviction that Scripture alone is the final authority for faith and life. The following statement summarizes the doctrinal foundation from which every article on Beyond TULIP is written.

I.

Our Foundation

We believe that the Holy Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments are the inspired, infallible, and authoritative Word of God. They are the final authority for all matters of faith, doctrine, and Christian living.

II.

God

We believe in one eternal God, existing in three Persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. These three are coequal, coeternal, and fully divine.

III.

Jesus Christ

We believe that Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God, conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary, fully God and fully man. He lived a sinless life, died on the cross as the atoning sacrifice for the sins of the world, rose bodily from the dead, ascended into heaven, and will return personally in power and glory.

IV.

The Holy Spirit

We believe that the Holy Spirit convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment. He regenerates all who believe in Christ, indwells every believer, empowers holy living, and equips the Church for service.

V.

Humanity

We believe that all people are created in the image of God but have fallen into sin through Adam. Every person is therefore a sinner by nature and by choice and is unable to save himself. Apart from God's grace, no one can be reconciled to God.

VI.

Salvation

We believe that salvation is entirely by God's grace through faith in Jesus Christ alone. Christ's atoning work is sufficient for every person and is offered genuinely to all through the gospel.

We believe that the gospel is God's appointed means by which He calls sinners to salvation. The Holy Spirit works through the proclamation of the gospel, enabling sinners to respond freely in repentance and faith. Faith is not a meritorious work but the God-appointed means of receiving His gracious gift of salvation.

We reject the idea that regeneration precedes faith. Scripture consistently presents faith in Christ as the condition for receiving eternal life and the new birth.

VII.

The Gospel Invitation

We believe that God sincerely desires all people to be saved and has commanded the gospel to be proclaimed to every nation. Every person is responsible before God for his or her response to the gospel.

VIII.

Election

We believe that God's election is according to His sovereign purpose and is centered in Jesus Christ. Those who are united to Christ by faith share in God's gracious election. Election magnifies God's grace without negating the genuine universal offer of the gospel or human responsibility.

IX.

The Church

We believe that the Church consists of all true believers in Jesus Christ. The local church exists to worship God, proclaim the gospel, make disciples, observe baptism and the Lord's Supper, and encourage believers in holy living.

X.

Last Things

We believe in the personal and visible return of Jesus Christ, the bodily resurrection of the dead, the final judgment, the everlasting blessedness of the redeemed, and the everlasting conscious punishment of the unrepentant.

XI.

Our Purpose

This website exists to examine Calvinist theology in light of Scripture through careful exegesis, historical theology, and respectful dialogue. Our goal is not to promote division but to encourage believers to search the Scriptures diligently and to proclaim the gospel faithfully, always seeking to glorify Jesus Christ alone.
